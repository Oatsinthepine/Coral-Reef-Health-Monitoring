Mapping Coral Reef Health in the Great Barrier Reef

Long-term coral condition, benthic cover, disturbance and protected-area context

This interactive narrative visualisation communicates selected findings from the Data Exploration Project (DEP) using long-term Australian Institute of Marine Science (AIMS) reef monitoring data, recent coral health context, and spatial protected-area information. Together, the views below explore where reefs are monitored, how live and dead coral cover has changed over time, how benthic composition shifts, how ecological indicators vary by sector, and how condition and disturbance measures relate to one another.

How to use this visualisation

  • Select a sector to update linked charts.
  • Hover charts to inspect values.
  • Click legends to focus layers.
  • Click heatmap cells to inspect relationships.

Data note

Charts draw on AIMS long-term monitoring records joined with reef coordinates and marine protected-area context. Benthic cover indicators are available for a subset of reef-year records; missing values are excluded from benthic and correlation views. Sector codes follow AIMS management sectors across the Great Barrier Reef.

Benthic cover indicators

Hard coral, soft coral, and algae cover describe the benthic structure of monitored reefs. Hard coral represents reef-building habitat; soft coral adds structural diversity; algae can increase when grazing or coral loss shifts competitive balance. The stacked view will show how these three indicators change over time.

Cover values are stacked for visual comparison. They are yearly mean cover indicators and are not constrained to sum to 100%.

Hover bands to inspect yearly means.

Summary and references

Key messages

Key narrative takeaways will be added after charts are implemented—for example, how live coral and benthic indicators co-vary across sectors and time, and how protected-area context frames spatial patterns.

Data sources

  • AIMS Long-term Monitoring Program (reef-year indicators and coordinates)
  • MMP Coral 2024 (supplementary coral context)
  • CAPAD Marine 2024 (Collaborative Australian Protected Areas Database — marine areas)

Limitations

  • Benthic cover is available for a subset of reef-year records; charts using these fields exclude incomplete rows.
  • Key Ecological Features (KEF) were not used as a direct overlap layer in this visualisation.
  • Protected-area status describes spatial context only and does not imply causation of reef condition.
