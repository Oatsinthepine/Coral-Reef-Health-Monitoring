Mapping Coral Reef Health in the Great Barrier Reef

Long-term coral condition, benthic cover, disturbance and protected-area context analysis and visualisation

This interactive narrative visualisation selected findings from the Data Exploration Project (DEP) using long-term Australian Institute of Marine Science (AIMS) reef monitoring data, recent coral health observations, and marine protected-area (CAPAD) spatial context. Together, the views below explore where reefs are monitored, how live and dead coral cover have changed over time, how benthic cover indicators shift, how ecological patterns vary across management sectors, and how reef condition indicators relate to disturbance measures.

How to use this visualisation

  • Select a sector to update the linked charts and map.
  • Select a period to compare long-term and recent patterns.
  • Use the indicator menu to switch the sector-level bubble chart.
  • Hover charts and map points to inspect detailed values.
  • Click legends to focus individual layers where available.
  • Click heatmap cells to inspect relationships among indicators.

Data note

Charts draw on AIMS long-term reef monitoring records, benthic cover observations, reef coordinates, and marine protected-area context. Benthic cover indicators are available for a subset of reef-year records, so incomplete rows are excluded from benthic and correlation views. Sector codes follow AIMS management sectors across the Great Barrier Reef. The visualisation is intended for exploratory interpretation, not causal ecological inference.

Monitored reef locations

The map shows monitored reef locations across the Great Barrier Reef, coloured by management sector and overlaid with simplified marine protected-area boundaries. Hover over reef points to read reef names and protected-area status, or click a point to filter the linked charts by sector.

Live and dead coral cover over time

This chart shows long-term mean live coral and dead coral cover across monitored reefs. Live coral reflects reef-building capacity, while dead coral can indicate recent mortality or structural loss. Reading the two series together helps summarise broad condition changes for the selected sector and period.

Hover over the chart to read yearly means; click the legend to focus a series.

Benthic cover indicators

Hard coral, soft coral, and algae cover describe the benthic structure of monitored reefs. Hard coral represents reef-building habitat; soft coral adds structural diversity; algae can increase when grazing pressure or coral loss shifts competitive balance. The stacked area view shows how these cover indicators change over time.

Cover values are stacked for visual comparison. They are yearly mean cover indicators and are not constrained to sum to 100%.

Hover over a year to inspect all benthic cover indicators; click the legend to focus one layer.

Ecological indicator by sector

Reef condition varies across management sectors. Switch between hard coral, soft coral, and algae cover to compare sector-year patterns. Bubble size represents the number of reef-year observations contributing to each sector-year mean, helping show both ecological value and data support.

Use the indicator control above to change the measure; hover bubbles for sector-year details.

Relationships among indicators

The heatmap summarises overall associations among reef condition and disturbance indicators, including live coral, dead coral, crown-of-thorns starfish (COTS) counts, hard coral, soft coral, and algae cover. It provides a compact overview of which indicators tend to move together or in opposite directions.

Correlation indicates association, not causation.

Summary and references

Key messages

  • Monitored reef sites are distributed along the Great Barrier Reef and can be explored by AIMS management sector. The linked map and sector controls connectspatial context with temporal and ecological indicator views.
  • Mean live coral cover varies substantially across the 1993–2023 monitoring record. Period and sector filters reveal that long-term patterns are not uniform across the reef system.
  • Benthic cover indicators provide complementary ecological context. Hard coral, soft coral, and algae cover describe different aspects of reef structure, and their stacked view highlights broad composition shifts over time.
  • The sector-level bubble chart shows that ecological indicators differ across sectors and years. Bubble size also reminds the reader that some sector-year means are supported by more reef-year observations than others.
  • The correlation heatmap highlights relationships among reef condition and disturbance indicators. For example, live coral and hard coral cover tend to move together, while algae cover is negatively associated with several coral cover indicators.
  • Marine protected-area boundaries provide important spatial context, but protected status should be interpreted as contextual information rather than causal evidence of reef condition.

This dashboard shows that coral reef condition in the Great Barrier Reef is both temporally variable and spatially uneven. Long-term live coral cover fluctuates across the monitoring period, while benthic indicators reveal contrasting patterns between coral cover and algae cover. The sector-level bubble view highlights that ecological indicators do not change uniformly across the reef system. The correlation heatmap provides a compact overview of relationships among reef condition and disturbance indicators, while the map places monitored reef sites within their marine protected-area context. Together, the views support exploratory interpretation rather than causal claims.
